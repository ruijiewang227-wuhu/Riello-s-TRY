# Research Rules For Product Analysis

Use research when the user asks about a specific modern product, company, current feature, founder/developer statement, market comparison, or asks for "不要胡编".

## Source Priority

Prefer:
1. Official product pages, docs, blogs, release notes, help centers.
2. Founder/developer interviews, official talks, official social posts.
3. Credible demos, benchmark posts, engineering blogs.
4. Influential X/Twitter, YouTube, newsletters, or community posts, clearly labeled as opinion.
5. Chinese context sources when analyzing China fit: Chinese product docs, local user discussions, local competitors, regulations, and ecosystem behavior.

Avoid:
- Unsourced claims.
- Treating rumors as facts.
- Over-indexing on one viral post.
- Claiming a product has a feature unless verified.

## Evidence Pack

Before answering, gather a compact evidence pack:

```text
Sources:
1. [source title] - what it proves
2. [source title] - what it proves
3. [source title] - what it proves

Known facts:
- ...

Inferences:
- ...

Uncertain / needs caveat:
- ...
```

When source quality is weak, say so.

## Answer Rule

Separate:
- **事实**: supported by sources.
- **判断**: product interpretation.
- **推测**: plausible inference, clearly marked.

Do not use web research for stable generic concepts unless a current product detail or latest claim is involved.
